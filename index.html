Who am I?

I’m a fourth year electrical engineering student at SJSU with multiple internships and a social personality. When I’m not studying in the IEEE room at SJSU, you can find me skateboarding down garages, up in the mountains, or attempting to develop content.

Work

My first interview and internship was with Underwriters Laboratories, where I was an RF/EMC lab assistant. Honestly, I did not believe I had this internship until I got my first paid check, it was surreal. I learned so many soft and technical skills that helped kick started my career as an engineer. I was exposed to excel programming and to UL's automated scripts. I was part of writing the reports that were sent to FCC for verification. I learned a lot about radio frequencys, especially the different bands related to cellular technology.

My second internship was with Robert Bosch LLC, where I worked with their autnomous driving team. I remember during the first day of work, I was given a linux computer and I had no clue how to use it. Luckily, I caught on real quick, like the other things that were thrown at me. I learned how to use RViZ, an open source 3D visualizer for ROS files. I was also able to learn some C++, where I was able to implement markers and lines. I was partly responsible for the perception of our autonomous car.

And now, I'm with Silver Springs Networks.
